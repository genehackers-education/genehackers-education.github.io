Lesson 8

Clean water: sense it & solve it

Grades
5–6
Time
45–60 min (run 1–3 parts)
Group
Small groups
Est. cost
$15–25

A modular clean-water unit: students debate real contamination sources, see how an engineered biosensor detects a specific toxin, then design, test, and improve their own water filter. It connects synthetic biology to a local issue and to real iGEM research — run one part or all three.

What students do

  • Debate what makes water unsafe — natural vs. human causes
  • See how an engineered biosensor detects a specific toxin (arsenic)
  • Design, test, and improve their own water filter

What you'll need

A cut 2-liter bottle, activated charcoal, sand, gravel, cotton balls, and food coloring + soil for the "polluted" water. About $15–25 a class. Modular — run one part or all three.

Full quantities and costs are in the materials & cost sheet.
